How to run a mix

  • Load two tracks — BPM is auto-detected from the kick. Best results with four-on-the-floor / electronic.
  • SYNC on a deck matches its tempo to the other deck so the beats lock together.
  • Crossfade uses an equal-power curve — slide from A to B with no volume dip.
  • EQ swap: kill the Low on the incoming track, then trade basslines as you cross over — the pro transition.
  • Filter centre = off. Turn right to sweep highs (tension), left to muffle (lowpass).
  • RISER ▸ DROP sweeps a riser + ducks the bass over the chosen beats, then slams everything back in.
  • Echo Out = tempo-synced delay tail for cutting a track. Brake = vinyl-style stop on the playing decks.
  • Airhorn / siren ship with real samples and play on every horn, drop and double-drop. Load your own airhorn under FX Weapons to replace it (remembered between sessions).
  • Sound credits: "DJ airhorn" by pfranzen — freesound.org/s/528807 — CC0. "Siren" by sean_eryx — freesound.org/s/197559 — CC BY 4.0 (trimmed & transcoded to mono).
  • Audio runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded.
  • Online Crate: paste a direct audio URL, load a whole GitHub repo of tracks (owner/repo), search Jamendo (Creative Commons) with a free client_id, or search SoundCloud — all land in the crate for the autopilot.
  • SoundCloud needs your own client_id (free, from their developer portal). It loads download-enabled and progressive tracks as real files, so they get full BPM/key analysis and all mixing features. Stream-only/HLS and Go+ (major-label) tracks can't be decoded in-browser, and the API may need you to run over localhost for cross-origin requests to succeed.
  • Online tracks need the host to allow cross-origin fetch (CORS). GitHub raw works; some CDNs (incl. parts of Jamendo) may block it — those show a red dot. This works best when you open the saved file in a normal browser tab.
  • Auto-Pilot: Connect folder links a local folder (it's remembered, and Rescan picks up tracks you add later); or use + Files. BPMs analyze in the background (green dot = ready), then Start Party mixes the whole crate — not just two tracks.
  • The energy wave warms the room up, climbs to a peak around two-thirds in, then eases back down — the target BPM rides that curve.
  • Peak BPM sets the ceiling (warm-ups land ~20 BPM below it). Min/track is only a soft floor — once it's passed, the DJ waits for the right moment to leave (it is not a 30/60/90-second timer). Use Full for long, patient sets.
  • At peak energy the autopilot fires a riser+drop over the blend automatically. Hit Skip to force the next mix.
  • DJ brain: every track is analyzed for BPM, beat-grid, musical key (Camelot), drop points, an energy map and a vocal/lead map (300–3500 Hz). It only mixes on a phrase boundary that lands in a sparse zone — an outro, breakdown or instrumental — and it scores every candidate across the whole 16-beat blend, so it won't leave during a vocal or cut into a drop.
  • It mixes at the right moment, not on a clock. It lets the track reach its payoff (plays past the last drop), then slips out in the outro. The green flag on the on-air waveform marks the chosen exit; the faint magenta band along the bottom is where vocals/leads live (zones it avoids). The status line shows the reason (outro / breakdown / instrumental) and a countdown under Next.
  • The one time it will ride into a drop is the double drop — when it's been building and teasing toward a drop-on-drop. That's deliberate, exactly like the user asked.
  • Harmonic picks the next track in a compatible key. With Auto-FX on it teases — sneaking the next track's hook in for a few beats and snatching it back to build the want — then commits with a phrase-locked blend or a real drop-on-drop double drop.
  • ⚡ Epic = full crowd-killer mode. On top of Auto-FX it stutters a beat-roll build into double drops for that "rrrr-BOOM", throws an extra siren on big peaks, and now and then rewinds the banger — spinning the on-air track back and re-dropping it at the drop (not the intro), so the energy never dips. Use it when you want chaos; leave it off for a smoother set. (Turning Epic on also switches Auto-FX on.)
  • Mix tuning (live — adjust mid-set): Track length = how long tracks ride before mixing (short ▸ long / outro); Adventurous = smooth blends & tops layers ◂▸ more cuts, loop-builds & silence slams; FX intensity = how often it throws airhorns, sirens & riser-drops at peaks (0% = clean mixes); Blend length = crossfade overlap in beats (shorter = punchier). Changes apply from the next mix.
  • It picks a transition technique for each mix like a DJ would, now from a fuller repertoire: EQ blend and tops layer (slides the incoming in as hats/percussion first, then swaps the bass) for compatible keys; filter fade or a lush echo out (delay + reverb wash) for clashing keys or energy shifts; cut / slam and the dramatic silence cut (a beat of dead air, then a downbeat slam) for genre jumps; a loop build that stutter-rolls into the drop; a spinback (the megamix wind-back: an accelerating reverse zip, then the next drop slams on the downbeat); a festival drop swap (ride the outgoing's breakdown while the incoming runs its own build underneath, then kill the old track the instant the new drop lands); and a double drop when the drops line up. The Mix readout shows which it chose. Loop builds, spinbacks and silence cuts are used sparingly (more often in ⚡ Epic) since overusing them sounds gimmicky.
  • Blends are scheduled, not animated. Every fade, bass swap and filter sweep is pre-scheduled on the audio clock (sample-accurate ramps), so transitions stay silky even if the tab is backgrounded mid-mix. The bass swaps over ~2 beats at the phrase point — never two basslines fighting — and after each mix the tempo glides back onto the energy curve instead of snapping.
  • Festival brain. Pacing follows the energy curve — tracks ride long in the warm-up and mix quick at peak time, and at higher energy the incoming track enters on its own phrase grid so its first drop lands ~48 beats after it appears (no riding a 2-minute intro at 1am). At a big vocal moment it will occasionally cut the music for a bar so the room sings it back, then slam in with an impact. Big slams get a downlifter sweep so they never land dry, and in ⚡ Epic the loop build pitch-rides the outgoing up ~5% into the slam — the hands-up adrenaline shot.
  • ⛶ Stage (top of the page) — fullscreen, beat-reactive party visuals: a radial spectrum in deck colours, a kick-flash core synced to the on-air beat grid, now playing, the next-mix countdown, and every transition / DOUBLE DROP / crowd-sing called out huge on screen. Put it on the TV or projector; Esc exits.
  • Loudness-matched & limited. Every track's sustained loudness is measured during analysis and auto-trimmed toward one reference level, so a quiet rip never disappears next to a brickwalled banger. A master limiter catches stacked drops + FX before they can clip the PA.
  • Host tools: hit ⏭ on a request to play it next (jumps the whole queue, re-cues the idle deck immediately); sort the crate by name / BPM / key / 🔥 energy; your tuning, toggles & pad mappings persist between sessions; and the screen stays awake while the party runs.
  • 🩺 Diag (Controller row) — saves a diagnostics file: the last ~300 auto-DJ decisions (every mix, watchdog recovery, FX fire, error with stack trace) plus full deck/crate/engine state and any crash snapshot from a previous session. If the set ever does something weird, hit it right away and share the file. The engine also has a circuit breaker: if transitions ever start cascading (e.g. broken files), it halts the storm, holds the current track, and recovery cuts stay silent — no airhorn loops.
  • Watch it play. Every control the autopilot touches moves on screen — crossfader, the two tempo faders, the Low EQ bass-swap, volumes, the Filter sweep on a filter fade, the Echo Out light on an echo, and the RISER ▸ DROP build bar — so you can see exactly what it's doing.
  • FX Weapons — Rewind!! spins the on-air track backward and slams it back from the top (the rave encore). Double Drop tempo-locks both decks, cuts, and detonates both basslines at once.
  • MIDI controller (DDJ-200 & standard USB-MIDI) — plug a controller in over USB, click Connect MIDI, and allow the browser prompt. The DDJ-200's faders, EQ, filter, jog, play/cue/sync and the performance pads (→ airhorn / siren / rewind / double-drop) map to the decks automatically. Two catches from the browser, not the app: it only works in Chrome or Edge (no Safari/iOS), and Web MIDI is blocked on file:// — so the saved file alone can't connect. The fix is one double-click: run Start-SUBVERTER.bat (Windows) or start-subverter.sh (Mac/Linux) from this folder — it serves the app on localhost (no install, no admin rights) and opens your browser. Hosted (e.g. GitHub Pages) it just works. Once you've allowed MIDI on an address, the controller auto-reconnects on load — no clicking Connect each session. Bluetooth won't work: the DDJ-200's Bluetooth is Pioneer's proprietary WeDJ protocol, not standard MIDI, so only its USB connection is usable here.
  • Airhorn / Siren are instant rave stabs. Beat Roll (hold) stutter-loops a 1, ½, or ¼ beat of the on-air deck — perfect for the "brrrrap" right before a drop.
  • DDJ-200 full map — Jogs nudge (hold SHIFT + jog to search/seek); tempo, 3-band EQ, CFX filter, channel faders & crossfader all live. Play, Cue (SHIFT+Cue jumps to start), Beat Sync. Performance pads top row = Airhorn / Siren / Rewind / Double-drop; bottom row = Beat Roll 1 / ½ / ¼ (hold) + Riser▸Drop. SHIFT + pads = MC Drop / Epic / Skip / Start-Stop / Transition. TRANSITION FX button triggers a mixed blend (or Skips while the autopilot's driving); headphone CUE 1/2 hard-cut the crossfader to that deck, MASTER centres it.
